#+title: Changelog — workflow-kit

* 0.7.1 — [2026-07-23]

Red-run self-tick guard, retro-born (bear-cub gamification retro, 2026-07-17). Before this release the only integrity language at the verification boundary was phase-close step 1's "never fake a result", scoped to manual PRD gate criteria — nothing anywhere stated what a session does when an automated check stays red and completion pressure invites reading the criterion loosely enough to tick the item anyway. Now stated in all three files that hold a verification boundary, one pass. story-closeout carries the primary rule: a red verify run is never self-ticked — when the check backing an item (the declared ~Verify command:~, a test run, or the check the item itself states) fails and the fix loop cannot make it green, the item is not marked done and its bar is not reinterpreted, narrowed, or substituted to clear the failing result; the failure is surfaced to the human, who may rule a documented deferral, and only that ruling makes =[-]= legitimate for a red-run item, with the ruling recorded in the item's LOGBOOK entry alongside the failing evidence. story-verifier's hard rules gain the matching audit posture: the bar is audited as written, never reinterpreted or weakened to reach a verdict, and a failing run is NOT MET however near the miss. phase-close step 1 extends its existing "never fake a result" sentence (untouched, per the crown-jewel protection on STOP-adjacent gate language) with the same rule for the automated checks: a red run is never recorded as passing by reinterpretation, and a failure unfixable within that step's bounds is surfaced for a human deferral ruling recorded in the gate record, never silently absorbed. The rule applies uniformly at every weight class — it is a prohibition, not ceremony, so there is nothing for personal-mvp to opt out of. Dry read-through against bear-cub's gamification stories confirmed the "check the item itself states" phrasing matches how real AC items are written. No hook changes; ~test/hooks_test.sh~ re-run and confirmed green (45/45) anyway.

* 0.7.0 — [2026-07-23]

The Decision Log lifts from per-initiative DESIGN sections to a repo-level shared log, and D-entries become linkable headings — the two paired backlog items (captured 2026-07-11), shipped together per the groom ruling so the same section changes once, not twice. Both implement the human ruling recorded 2026-07-11/12 (single repo-wide monotonic D-sequence; chain-qualified pointers; eager-notice/lazy-repair/owner-only-writes overrule protocol; concurrent-mint collision accepted as rare and gate-fixable) — the ruling is the authorization for the crown-jewel edits to org-conventions' Decision Log section; every jewel sentence the ruling does not force stays verbatim.

Location: ~docs/decisions.org~, one monotonic D-sequence across all initiatives, appended newest last, seeded by project-setup and copied in by both README install paths. The lift moves the location convention only, matching bear-cub's interim state, which already runs the single sequence but stores entries inside each initiative's DESIGN: historical entries remain in situ in their chains, list-format, no retroactive surgery; the shared log starts at the next number with a header pointing back at the historical ranges. The DESIGN template's Decision Log section is now pointer-only — entries are never minted in the DESIGN itself. That was the one fork the ruling left open (pointer vs. local-entries-plus-shared-log); pointer-only wins because local entries would duplicate the record, and the kit's own single-source-of-truth invariant settles that fork.

Format: each entry is a heading (~D27 (2026-07-12) — terse title~) with a =:CUSTOM_ID:= of the lowercase D-number, so citations are real org links instead of grep targets — from a chain document =[[file:../decisions.org::#d27][D27]]=, within the log =[[#d27][D27]]=. org-conventions carries a worked example showing the entry heading, the CUSTOM_ID drawer, and an Advisory line citing it as a link. Historical list-format entries keep their bare =(Dx)= citations — the link convention applies from adoption forward. Once the log serves more than one chain, "settles" targets and → § pointers are chain-qualified with the initiative slug ("settles extras/SC-2", "→ extras/Key Behaviors") — bare "FR-13"/"§6" is ambiguous across initiatives.

Cross-chain rules land in org-conventions per the ruling: the overrule protocol (a session overruling a decision another chain relies on owes exactly one thing — a loud supersession entry in the shared log, with the old D-number cited as a link and an advisory-style payload wherever the dead position could trap; each open chain reconciles at its own gates; no cross-chain writes by non-owning sessions), and the accepted collision risk (two concurrent sessions minting the same next number — rare solo, repaired at the gate by renumbering the later-committed entry, a repair that mints nothing). The mutability policy gains a ~docs/decisions.org~ bullet (append-only; the gate's duplicate-number fix is the only sanctioned repair), and the docs-in-VC section notes the shared log rides in the doc commit of the pass that minted its entry.

Consuming skills re-pointed: update-design step 2(a) mints into the shared log with chain-qualified pointers and carries the overrule doorbell; phase-close gains a shared-decision-log pass (new step 5 — lazy repair lands at this chain's own gate: supersessions affecting this chain route through retroactive update-design like any divergence, and duplicate numbers are renumbered here); promote-design mints design-time entries into the shared log and its brainstorm-reference scrub now covers the promotion's new shared-log entries; both CLAUDE.md templates name ~docs/decisions.org~ in the Document chain section. The ruling's parked horizon question (does the DESIGN body eventually lift to a repo-level living system description?) stays parked — nothing in this release answers it. No hook changes; ~test/hooks_test.sh~ re-run and confirmed green anyway.

* 0.6.1 — [2026-07-22]

Three hand-sized (inert-edit) items, all retro/real-use born. First, proven twice in live use on the extras initiative (the update-design invocation after story 01; the initiative-close brief): stating the expected outcome of a skill run in the invocation, before it runs, turns the run into a checkable prediction, and deviations from the prediction become signal instead of noise. README gains one paragraph near the Brainstorm kickoff fence naming this pattern, with the concrete update-design example ("expected: PLAN reconciliation only, NO DESIGN body edits; if you believe a body edit is warranted, surface the case before making it"). This is documented as a human practice only — deliberately not encoded into any skill, since it only works when the human states the expectation.

Second, from the bear-cub gamification retro (2026-07-17): a session that batch misattributed and silently deleted the human's uncommitted scratch code. Both project-setup CLAUDE.md templates (=.mvp= and =.workgrade=) gain a Workflow-section rule closing that gap: an implementation session refuses to start work over a dirty working tree, and unexplained working-tree state is surfaced to the human, never classified-and-fixed by the session itself.

Third, authority marking for D-entries vs. session-authored elaboration, from the same retro: that batch, an implementation shape that lived only in session-authored Context-bullet prose got defended downstream with decision-grade authority ("the design specifies…") when no Decision Log entry had ruled on it. org-conventions' Decision Log section gains a closing note — only what a D-entry records carries decision authority; body prose beyond that is session-authored implementation guidance, and yields to owner preference without ceremony. promote-design gets the same rule plus the attribution discipline it governs: canon is defended as canon with the citation actually checked; a session-authored choice is defended as a choice, on the merits of the specific proposal at hand, not the category it resembles. Counterweight carried alongside, per human ruling: un-ruled positions are not thereby indefensible — pushback that keeps work on track is valued; the discipline governs what the defense is made of, not whether to defend.

* 0.6.0 — [2026-07-16]

Docs-in-VC policy. org-conventions gains a "Document chain & version control" section: chain documents are committed in doc-only commits, never mixed with code changes; they enter version control only at canonical moments — the PRD at lock, the DESIGN at canon approval, and thereafter each update-design pass (D-entry + body edit + Advisory) as its own doc commit — with working drafts and intermediates never committed at all; and the brainstorm spec is never committed, since it is scratch material the DESIGN supersedes, with ~docs/superpowers/specs/~ permanently gitignored. project-setup gains a ~.gitignore~ template carrying that pattern, README's plugin and copy-in install steps now append it to the target repo's =.gitignore=, and both CLAUDE.md templates' Document chain section notes the specs directory as transient, uncommitted territory.

That policy exposed a dangling-reference risk the moment it landed: a Decision Log entry's `→ §` pointer citing a brainstorm-spec section by number goes stale once the spec is never committed — proven on bear-cub's gamification DESIGN, which cited brainstorm spec §6.2 and §6.3 in D44 and D45. Two fixes follow. First, org-conventions' Decision Log spec — a verbatim crown jewel under this repo's CLAUDE.md — gains one additive bullet, human-authorized for this release and word-for-word as given (no existing jewel text reworded): `→ §` pointers target PRD or DESIGN sections only; a rejected alternative that originated in the brainstorm is restated in words within the entry, never cited by spec section. Second, create-prd and promote-design each gain a scrub step — create-prd's runs immediately before the draft-then-rename handoff, promote-design's immediately before canon approval — that greps the draft (and, for promote-design, the Decision Log entries too) for "spec §", "brainstorm", and the spec filename pattern, reports every hit, and rewrites each to self-contained wording per the new org-conventions section rather than restating the rule locally. Both are report-then-fix steps: list what was found and how it was reworded, never a silent rewrite.

Marketplace manifest tightened to match: ~.claude-plugin/marketplace.json~'s marketplace name changed from ~dustin-plugins~ to ~dustin~, description trimmed to the same framing as ~plugin.json~'s; README's plugin-install instructions updated to match the new =/plugin install workflow-kit@dustin= form. No hook changes in this release; ~test/hooks_test.sh~ re-run and confirmed green anyway.

* 0.5.1 — [2026-07-13]

Line-initial-asterisk rule. Real project use surfaced a silent corruption bug: a LOGBOOK entry quoting Elixir error text had a line beginning with ~*~, which org-mode parsed as a level-2 heading and split the document mid-structure at an extras close — caught only because a human happened to notice the break during review, not because anything flagged it. org-conventions gains a rule for this, placed after Inline markup so it applies to all body and LOGBOOK writing rather than living inside one drawer's spec: no line in body or LOGBOOK text may begin with an asterisk unless it is intentionally an org heading; quoted code or error output that starts a line with ~*~ must be reflowed so the asterisk lands mid-sentence, or wrapped in a ~#+begin_src~/~#+begin_example~ block. The rule states its own self-check verbatim: run ~grep '^\*\*'~ on the file before finishing any org write.

* 0.5.0 — [2026-07-12]

Six-item patch from real-project friction, dependency-ordered. PRD Success Criteria are now a numbered list (~templates/PRD.org~, with a comment that the numbers are stable handles — never renumbered, an amendment retires rather than reassigns); create-prd states the numbering; user-stories' AC drift guard traces an AC's intent to a specific PRD Success Criterion cited as SC-n rather than the section in general. This was the prerequisite for the next fix: an extras promote-design run had cleared brainstorm/PRD alignment via provenance phrasing ("the PRD was created from it, so no divergence") that a human had to re-derive by hand to trust — a compliance claim indistinguishable from its own absence isn't a check. promote-design's closing step now requires the demonstration itself before DESIGN.org goes up for approval: a criterion-by-criterion SC-n → DESIGN section trace, plus a disposition line (faithful copy / rejected-alternative D-entry / dropped) for every brainstorm position the PRD overrode, with an example block; absence of the trace counts as the check not having happened, at every weight class. Input resolution stopped guessing too: promote-design's "newest under ~docs/superpowers/specs/~" mtime fallback — silently wrong under concurrent initiatives, and perturbable by an unrelated ~mv~ — is replaced with "list candidates and ask," mirrored into create-prd so both skills share one posture; no "newest" language remains in either skill's input resolution.

Brainstorm sessions genuinely drift into design altitude before a PRD exists (one real run produced a 250-line brainstorm document with schema and module names already decided) — survivable downstream since promote-design and the PRD filter absorb it, but confident pre-contract material is still a smell worth fencing. Superpowers owns the brainstorming skill itself, so the kit ships the fence for a human to paste instead of editing that skill: README gains a "Brainstorm kickoff fence" section holding the main thread at outcome altitude and parking mechanism material in a "for the design doc" section; create-prd gains one sentence naming that section as ordinary feedstock for promote-design, not something it extracts.

user-stories' single-open-batch rule was protective-only with no escape hatch: real evidence surfacing mid-batch (a bear-cub story invalidating a prior deferral) had no sanctioned path but the STOP. The rule now has exactly one exception — explicit human authorization for the run, quoted back to the human, with a LOGBOOK note on the batch heading recording the insertion and its story link, the evidence motivating it, and the authorization — while the original STOP sentence for the unauthorized case is untouched, still the only path reached when that authorization is absent.

Last, the Definition of Done's verification item and phase-close step 1 assumed a fixed mix invocation (~mix check~ for work-grade; ~mix format --check-formatted~ and ~mix test~ for personal-mvp) — bear-cub's real gate is ~mix precommit~, papered over with an ad hoc CLAUDE.md sentence rather than a first-class convention. Both project-setup templates now carry an optional ~Verify command:~ line under Stack & commands, defaulted to whichever invocation each weight class already ran so existing repos without the line are unaffected; the DoD blocks in user-stories, phase-close step 1, and story-closeout's Definition-of-Done evidence step all read that declared line and name its default instead of hard-coding a command.

* 0.4.2 — [2026-07-12]

Org inline-markup convention. Model-authored chain documents defaulted to =verbatim= for all inline monospace, ignoring org's semantic distinction between code and literal strings. org-conventions gains an Inline markup rule alongside the timestamp rule: =~code~= for anything a language would parse (identifiers, module/function names, expressions, shell commands, file paths), =verbatim= only for literal non-code strings quoted exactly (stored values, config keys, log output); prefer =~code~= when unsure. Audited the kit's own shipped skills, templates, and agents against the new rule; found one violation, in the Advisories example block inside org-conventions/SKILL.md itself — =async: true=, =ecto_sqlite3=, and =busy_timeout= were marked =verbatim= where they're code. Corrected the markup delimiters only, wording untouched, honoring the block's crown-jewel protection in CLAUDE.md. No sweep of existing project chain documents; story excerpts stay verbatim-faithful to the DESIGN they quote, markup warts included.

Brainstorm-document lineage rules. * 0.4.0 — [2026-07-11]

promote-prd renamed to create-prd. The prd-lock hook is approval-blind: it unconditionally blocks Write/Edit/MultiEdit on any =*PRD.org= path and write-shaped Bash (redirects, =sed -i=, =tee=, =mv=/=cp= onto, =rm=, =truncate=) targeting =PRD.org=, so the skill's own "write PRD.org" step would have been blocked by the kit's own hook on first live use, and the revision loop was impossible once the file existed. Fixed: the skill now writes only to =PRD-draft.org= — a filename that matches neither the hook's filename glob nor its Bash regex, confirmed against hooks/prd-lock.sh directly — and iterates on the draft with the human without restriction. Approval is now the human's own rename, =mv .../PRD-draft.org .../PRD.org=, run in their own shell; from that moment the file sits under prd-lock and is the locked contract (a draft-then-rename flow). promote-design gains a guard: if the initiative directory holds PRD-draft.org but no PRD.org, STOP rather than read the draft as if it were the approved contract. hooks/prd-lock.sh itself is unchanged; hooks_test.sh gains cases for drafts passing the hook and mv/cp onto PRD.org still blocking. Renamed repo-wide: skill directory, frontmatter, README pipeline table, both project-setup CLAUDE.md variants, plugin.json description.

* 0.3.2 — [2026-07-11]

Advisories gain an Issuance rule, closing the gap left by the payload litmus: the litmus said what an advisory must contain, not when one should be written at all. An advisory is now issued only where a superseded position could trap a future reader — never as a mutation record; additive or neutral body edits get no entry, since git and the gate's reconciliation pass already own mutation completeness. A "Sections affected" line may name a section only if that section's superseded position is stated in the payload — naming an affected section without stating what it used to say is the litmus failure in disguise. Ratified by human ruling, added verbatim to org-conventions' Advisories section.

* 0.3.0 — [2026-07-11]

Two-log structure, batch/gate ontology, phase-close slimmed to gate mechanics. The single =* Changelog= is replaced by two headings with distinct referents: =* Decision Log= (append-only, Dn-numbered judgments about the system being built) and =* Advisories= (superseded positions preserved for cold readers of the document), cross-cited by D-number. org-conventions defines both, with the payload litmus (FROM-first) and the paired invariants — an Advisory without a body edit, and a post-canon D-entry without a paired Advisory, are both suspect. Phases are demoted to batches: a batch is the unit that ends at a gate, "Phase N" is an optional label, and the gate record (a LOGBOOK entry on the batch heading) doubles as the closed-marker — the open batch is the one without a gate record. PLAN.org carries batch headings; user-stories appends a new batch and refuses to open one over an unclosed batch. phase-close is rewritten to gate mechanics only — verification (plus PRD-declared gate criteria), behavior/PLAN audit that routes repairs back through update-design, advisory withdrawal/promotion, and the gate record; its old v2-candidate promotion and stages-earned-their-keep retrospective move to the (not-yet-built) initiative-close skill. update-design's pivot handling becomes a three-step (mint D-entry → body edit → Advisory). New CLAUDE.md invariant: conversation-born terms don't ship except as parenthetical mnemonics after the rule they compress. Stale =references/org-conventions.md= paths corrected to the skill. hooks_test.sh gains a Changelog-string tripwire over skills/templates/project-setup.
